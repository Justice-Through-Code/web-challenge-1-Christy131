A Shropshire Lad

'Lovliest of trees, the cherry now is hung with the bloom \n along the bough, And stands about the woodland reide, wearing white for \n for Eastertide.'

'Now of my threescore years and ten, Twenty will not come again,\n and take from sevemty springs a score, it only leaves me fifty more.'

And since to look on things in bloom fifty springs are little room,\n about the woodlands I will go to see the cherry hung with snow.
